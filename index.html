About

Research Leadership

I lead the New Lineages of Life Group at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (DOE JGI/LBNL), where we discover novel bacterial, archaeal, eukaryotic microbes, and viruses in environmental sequence data. My work combines cutting-edge machine learning with multi-omics approaches to expand our understanding of microbial diversity and develop pathogen-agnostic detection systems for biosecurity applications.

As Biosecurity Lead at DOE JGI, I translate fundamental microbiome research into practical tools for threat detection and characterization, working closely with defense agencies.

Research Overview

Giant Virus Biology

Discovering and characterizing nucleocytoplasmic large DNA viruses (NCLDV) through global metagenomics. My work has expanded known giant virus diversity by over 11-fold and revealed viruses with unprecedented genomic complexity, including translation system components.

AI/ML Applications

Developing machine learning tools for pathogen detection, genome classification, and microbiome analysis. Projects include neural network-based phylogenetics, symbiont classification systems, and pathogen-agnostic detection models.

Novel Microbial Diversity

Leading the New Lineages of Life initiative to systematically expand our understanding of bacterial, archaeal, and eukaryotic diversity through large-scale metagenomics and single-cell genomics approaches.

Pathogen Detection & Biosecurity

Translating microbiome research into biosecurity applications, developing pathogen-agnostic detection methods and risk assessment frameworks for emerging biological threats.
